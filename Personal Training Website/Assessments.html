Creating your fitness

FUNCTIONAL MOVEMENT ASSESSMENT

A functional movement assessment or FMS assesses 7 fundamental movement patterns to see if you are at risk of causing injury to yourself during sporting activity or daily lifestyle activities. Its important to move right especially if you intend to start doing some form of resistance training

FLEXIBILITY ASSESSMENT

Depending on your goals and what you do day to day dictactes will dictate your flexability goals and requirements. However whatever the ROM you require around a joint it is fundamental that balance is achieved through both sides of the body

MUSCULAR FITNESS

Muscular power, strength and endurance is required in different ratios dependant on the sport. Emphasis should be tailored for your requirements and training goals. Undulating the volume, intensity and repetitions, with the main emphasis still in mind, will hit all fitness components and will keep training interesting and non tedious. undulating training loads can also help prevent fatigue as you'll be training different energy system allowing recovery inbetween each one.

A one rep max can determine your current strength level and can be used as a baseline to programme each muscular fitness component

CARDIOVASCULAR FITNESS

The amount of oxygen a person can take in and utilise is a measure of VO2. Cardiovascular training emphasises the stress put on the heart, lungs and training can reduce the liklihood of many diseases and can improve athletic performance. Hypertrophy happens in a muscle and the heart is a muscle which can be thickened and strengthened to work more efficently

NUTRITION

Nutrition should take equal responsibility when the goal is to feel fitter and healthier. Hitting the right macro and micro nutrients needed for YOUR body is essential for your body to perform physiological functions and to repair and recover the body after stress and training
